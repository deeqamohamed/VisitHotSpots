Liverpool


Once one of the largest and most important ports in Europe, Liverpool has long been a centre of trade and culture. While its industrial days may be over, Liverpool’s cultural and historical significance lives on, making it an ideal place to visit for a long-weekend. The city boasts a range of fascinating and historically important museums, a dreamy skyline, stunning architecture and, of course, a humming nightlife. Whether you are looking for long walks along the dock, to brush up on your local knowledge or to dance the night away, you can find it right here in Liverpool!



FOOD & DRINKS



Liverpool has a vast range of great restaurants, from Italian, to Mexican, to traditional English pub grub. For the foodies, we recommend a stroll around the Georgian Quarter, which has plenty of tasteful and unfussy bistros to wind down in. Check out Papillon on Hope Street for simple, elegant food and a buzzing atmosphere. For the night owls, make sure to check out the bars in the Baltic Triangle (fun fact: Peaky Blinders was filmed around there!). Or you could try your luck at speakeasy Berry & Rye for inventive cocktails, live piano and a chilled vibe. Just make sure to knock at the right door…



ACCOMMODATION



There is a whole host of accomodation choices in and around Liverpool, ranging from budget to blowout. For sightseers on a budget, the Travelodge on the Strand is bang in the centre of town; only a 3 minutes walk from the Albert Dock and its array of cultural attractions, museums and restaurants. For thos looking for a little more me-time, try the Hope Street Hotel, which offers spa treatments and stunning views of the city. There are also plenty of AirBnb options to explore, to find something tailored to your stay.



ACTIVITIES



It wouldn’t be a weekend in Liverpool without a visit to the Cavern Club – birthplace of The Beatles! Grab a drink, listen to some live music and immerse yourself in Beatle Mania! For the shopaholics there are plenty of places to get your fix at Liverpool One, and from there it is a short, pleasant walk to the dock. Built in the 1800s, the Albert Dock is an impressive space lined with old industrial warehouses and home to several engaging museums and galleries. We recommend the Museum of Liverpool to get to know some more about Scouse history and culture. You can also hop on the ferry across the Mersey to get a glimpse of the Liverpool skyline in all its glory!
